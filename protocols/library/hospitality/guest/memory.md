## Static — Authored Domain Knowledge

### The Market Context

The AI industry is transitioning from "AI as tool" (2023-2025) to "AI as communication platform" (2025+). First-generation products told professionals to learn prompting or get replaced. The market is fatigued by AI hype and skeptical of another chat wrapper. Visitors arrive with cautious interest or active skepticism. Both are productive — the skeptic who engages deeply often converts harder than the enthusiast who bounces.

### The Inversion Thesis

The sell is not "AI is powerful." Everyone knows that. The sell is "you control it." The inversion — domain experts drive the protocol, programmers build the infrastructure — resonates strongest with:
- Professionals who felt threatened by AI v1 messaging
- Business owners who tried ChatGPT and couldn't figure out how to deploy it for clients
- Technical evaluators who built custom LLM integrations and felt the infrastructure pain

### What Converts

**High-conversion:** Visitor describes a specific use case → map to pack → paint the morning-after → Contact Data Rail. Pricing questions after 3+ substantive turns. Competitor mentions → architectural comparison → differentiation lands. "Can it do X?" where X maps to an existing capability → demonstrate live.

**Low-conversion:** Technology-only questions without a business problem. Immediate free-tier requests. 10+ shallow questions without depth on any topic.

### Common Objections

- **"How is this different from ChatGPT?"** → Governance, deliverables, dashboard, vault. "ChatGPT is a consumer product. This is business infrastructure."
- **"We already built something with LangChain/OpenAI"** → "You rebuilt state, routing, auth, and compliance from scratch before building the thing you actually wanted. That's the problem we solve."
- **"Is this just a chatbot?"** → Four-surface distinction (Desk/Deliverable/Dashboard/Vault). Chatbots produce conversation. TMOS13 produces deliverables.
- **"Why should I trust a startup?"** → Test suite, compliance posture, SOC 2 progress, on-premise option. "Try the Preview."
- **"AI hallucinates"** → Protocol-driven, not prompt-driven. Business rules are deterministic. Routing is deterministic. Deliverables are structured, not freeform.

### Visitor Archetypes

- **The Evaluator** — Comparing solutions. Wants capabilities, pricing, proof. Give substance fast. Four-surface flow is the differentiator.
- **The Builder** — Developer/CTO. Wants SDK, API, deployment. Speak their language. They'll sell it internally if the architecture is sound.
- **The Skeptic** — Burned by AI v1 hype. Engage directly. The live demo is your best weapon.
- **The Dreamer** — Vision but no technical background. The Pack Builder is the answer.
- **The Investor** — Evaluating the business. Thesis, defensibility, growth model. Don't pitch features — pitch architecture.
- **The Regulated** — Healthcare, legal, financial. Lead with on-premise, local AI, HIPAA, data sovereignty.

### What Questions Come First

In order of frequency:
1. "What is this?" / "What does TMOS13 do?"
2. "How much does it cost?"
3. "How is this different from [competitor]?"
4. "Can it do [specific thing]?"
5. "How does it work technically?"
6. "Is it secure?"
7. "Who built this?"

The first question sets the trajectory. An evaluator who opens with pricing is further along than one who opens with "what is this." Calibrate accordingly.
