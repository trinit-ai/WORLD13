## Static — Authored Domain Knowledge

### Campaign Type Routing

Marketing campaigns cluster into predictable archetypes. The first few sentences from a user almost always signal the campaign type:

- **Product launch** — "launching," "new product," "release," "go-to-market," "GTM," "announcing"
- **Brand awareness** — "brand," "awareness," "visibility," "recognition," "top of mind," "thought leadership"
- **Lead generation** — "leads," "pipeline," "signups," "demos," "conversions," "funnel"
- **Event promotion** — "event," "webinar," "conference," "workshop," "launch event," "meetup"
- **Retention / re-engagement** — "churn," "win back," "re-engage," "inactive," "loyalty," "renewal"
- **Content marketing** — "blog," "SEO," "organic," "content strategy," "editorial," "inbound"

### What Makes a Good Campaign Brief

A campaign brief that actually gets executed needs: (1) a single clear objective, (2) a defined audience with specifics, (3) a positioning statement that passes the "so what" test, (4) 2-4 channels with rationale, (5) a content plan with formats and cadence, (6) a timeline with milestones, (7) success metrics that are measurable.

**The handoff test:** Can a marketing manager or creative team read this brief and start execution within a day without follow-up questions? If yes, the brief succeeded.

### Channel Effectiveness Patterns

- **Paid search** — High intent, immediate ROI signal. Works when demand already exists. Poor for category creation.
- **Paid social** — Audience targeting strength. Good for awareness and consideration. Requires creative rotation.
- **Organic social** — Trust building, community. Slow build, compounding returns. Requires consistency over months.
- **Email** — Highest ROI per dollar for owned audiences. Useless without a list. Segmentation is everything.
- **Content / SEO** — Long-tail compounding. 3-6 month payoff. Best for established authority or niche topics.
- **Influencer / creator** — Audience borrowing. Fast reach. Expensive per engagement. Authenticity dependent.
- **Events** — High-touch, high-cost. Best for enterprise, community building, or product demos. Hard to scale.
- **PR / media** — Credibility and reach. Unpredictable. Best as amplifier, not primary channel.
- **Partnerships** — Audience overlap leverage. Slow to negotiate. High ROI when aligned. Requires mutual value.
- **Direct mail** — Pattern interrupt in a digital world. Works for high-value B2B, local, or luxury. Expensive per touch.

### Audience Segmentation Principles

- **Behavioral > demographic.** What people do matters more than what they are. "Searched for CRM software in the last 30 days" beats "35-50 year old business owner."
- **Segment by problem, not product.** People don't wake up wanting your category. They wake up with a problem. Find the problem segments.
- **ICP vs. TAM.** Ideal Customer Profile is not Total Addressable Market. Start with the 200 people who would cry if your product disappeared. Build outward.
- **Negative personas matter.** Who you're NOT targeting saves as much budget as who you are. Define the anti-audience.

### Budget Allocation Heuristics

- **70/20/10 rule:** 70% on proven channels, 20% on emerging bets, 10% on experiments.
- **Never spread thin.** One channel done well beats four channels done poorly. If budget is under $5K/month, pick one primary and one supporting channel max.
- **Creative is not free.** Budget for production -- ad creative, copy, design, video. Half of campaign budgets should be creative if the team is small.
- **Measurement costs money.** Attribution tools, analytics, tracking infrastructure. Budget 5-10% for measurement and reporting.

### Common Client Behaviors

- Users often start with a channel ("I want to run Facebook ads") instead of an objective. Redirect to "What outcome are you after?" first.
- Budget ranges are often aspirational. When someone says "$10K/month," probe whether that's committed or hoped-for.
- Timeline pressure is usually external -- a launch date, a board meeting, a seasonal window. Surface the deadline early.
- Many users conflate tactics with strategy. "We need a social media strategy" usually means "we need a marketing strategy that includes social."
- Teams of one overestimate their production capacity. A realistic content plan for a solo marketer is 2-3 pieces per week, not 2-3 per day.
