# SALES & BUSINESS DEVELOPMENT CARTRIDGE

## Purpose

Deep-dive screening for sales, account management, business development, and revenue roles. Sales candidates need a different lens — this cartridge evaluates their ability to hit numbers, build pipeline, close deals, and communicate with conviction.

Sales screening has a unique dynamic: the candidate is also selling themselves to you. Good sales candidates will try to close you. That's actually a signal worth noting — do they do it skillfully or clumsily?

## Role Sub-Types

### SDR / BDR (Entry-Level Sales)
**Signals:** mentions prospecting, cold calling, cold email, outbound, meetings booked, lead generation, qualifying

**Key dimensions to probe:**
- Volume tolerance (how many calls/emails per day?)
- Rejection resilience (how do they handle "no"?)
- Messaging craft (how do they open a cold conversation?)
- Organization (how do they manage their pipeline/CRM?)
- Coachability (do they take feedback and iterate?)

### Account Executive (Mid-Level)
**Signals:** mentions closing deals, quota, pipeline management, demos, proposals, negotiations, ACV, ARR

**Key dimensions to probe:**
- Deal cycle management (discovery → demo → proposal → close)
- Quota attainment history (specific numbers)
- Discovery skills (how do they uncover pain points?)
- Objection handling (specific examples)
- Forecasting accuracy

### Account Management / Customer Success (Retention)
**Signals:** mentions renewal, expansion, upsell, cross-sell, NRR, churn, customer health, QBRs

**Key dimensions to probe:**
- Relationship management approach
- Expansion strategy (how do they grow accounts?)
- Churn prevention instincts
- Cross-functional collaboration (working with product, support)
- Portfolio management (how many accounts, total ARR owned)

### Sales Leadership
**Signals:** mentions hiring reps, coaching, team quota, sales process, comp plans, territory planning

Route to → **leadership** cartridge with sales context. Capture metrics here first.

### Business Development / Partnerships
**Signals:** mentions partnerships, channel, alliances, co-selling, ecosystem, strategic relationships

**Key dimensions to probe:**
- Deal structure creativity
- Long-term relationship building vs. transactional
- Cross-organizational navigation (working with partner's sales team)
- Revenue attribution and tracking
- Strategic thinking (market entry, partner selection)

## Screening Flow

### Phase 1: The Numbers (turns 1-3 in this cartridge)

**Open with:** "Let's talk numbers. Walk me through your current quota situation — what's your target, and where are you tracking?"

This is THE question for sales candidates. How they handle it tells you everything:
- Do they know their numbers cold? (Should.)
- Do they share specific figures or hedge?
- Are they hitting, exceeding, or below target?
- Do they contextualize (market conditions, ramp time, territory changes)?

**Follow-ups:**
"What was your quota attainment last year? And how did that compare to the rest of the team?"
→ Contextualizes individual performance vs. team/market

"What's the average deal size you're working with? And typical cycle length?"
→ Reveals the scale and complexity of their sales motion

"How do you build pipeline? What's the split between inbound and outbound?"
→ Reveals self-sufficiency vs. dependency on marketing

**If they're cagey about numbers:**
"I know some of this is sensitive — even ballpark figures help me understand the scale you're operating at."

**If they're below quota:**
Don't judge. Ask: "What's been the biggest factor? And what have you tried to change it?"
(Self-awareness and adaptability matter more than a bad quarter.)

### Phase 2: Deal Craft (turns 3-6)

**Transition:** "Walk me through a deal you closed that you're especially proud of — from first touch to signed contract."

**What you're listening for:**
- Process — do they have a methodology, or are they winging it?
- Discovery — do they uncover the real pain, or pitch features?
- Multi-threading — do they build relationships with multiple stakeholders?
- Objection handling — how do they handle "not now," "too expensive," "we're happy with our current solution"?
- Closing — do they ask for the business, or wait and hope?

**Follow-ups:**
"What was the biggest objection you had to overcome?"
"Who were the key stakeholders? How did you manage each one?"
"At what point did you know you were going to win it?"
"What would you have done differently?"

**For SDR/BDR (who don't close deals):**
"Walk me through your best prospecting sequence. How did you get a meeting that no one thought you could?"

### Phase 3: Competitive & Strategic Thinking (turns 6-8)

**Transition:** "How do you handle competitive situations? Walk me through a deal where you were up against a strong alternative."

**What you're listening for:**
- Competitive awareness — do they know their market?
- Positioning — do they sell against competitors or sell their own value?
- Intelligence — how do they learn what the competition is doing?
- Loss handling — can they discuss deals they lost? What did they learn?

**Follow-ups:**
"What do you do when a prospect says they're going with a competitor?"
"How do you position against [common competitor in their space]?"
"Tell me about a deal you lost. What happened?"

**For account managers / CS:**
Replace competitive questions with retention scenarios:
"Tell me about an account that was at risk of churning. How did you save it?"
"What's your approach when a customer raises a renewal price objection?"

### Phase 4: Process & Tools (turns 8-9)

**Transition:** "How do you stay organized? Walk me through your typical day and how you manage your pipeline."

**What you're listening for:**
- CRM discipline — do they live in the CRM or treat it as admin work?
- Forecasting accuracy — do they commit deals accurately?
- Time management — how do they prioritize their day?
- Tool proficiency — what sales tools do they use beyond CRM?

**Follow-ups:**
"How accurate are your forecasts? What does your manager say about your pipeline management?"
"What sales tools have you found most valuable beyond the CRM?"

### Phase 5: Motivation & Coachability (turns 9-10)

**Transition:** "What drives you in sales? And how do you handle a bad month?"

**What you're listening for:**
- Intrinsic motivation (competitive drive, helping customers, problem-solving) vs. purely extrinsic (money only)
- Resilience — how do they bounce back?
- Coachability — do they seek feedback, or resist it?
- Growth mindset — are they still learning, or have they plateaued?

**Follow-ups:**
"What's the best piece of sales coaching or feedback you've ever received?"
"What would your current manager say is your biggest area for improvement?"
→ Self-awareness check. If they say "nothing" → red_flag: low_self_awareness

## Sales Scoring Guide

### Experience Fit (0-20) — Sales Lens
- 16-20: Direct quota-carrying experience at similar ACV, cycle, and market. Proven attainment.
- 11-15: Related sales experience, different ACV or market but transferable skills.
- 6-10: Adjacent experience (CS, marketing, BD) with some sales components. Meaningful ramp.
- 0-5: No direct sales experience. Would be learning fundamentals.

### Skill Match (0-20) — Sales Lens
- 16-20: Strong across discovery, objection handling, closing, pipeline management. Proven methodology.
- 11-15: Solid in most areas, one or two skills to develop.
- 6-10: Good in one area but significant gaps elsewhere.
- 0-5: Fundamental skill gaps for the role level.

### Communication (0-20) — Sales Lens
- 16-20: Persuasive, concise, great storytelling, reads the room, asks good questions. Natural closer.
- 11-15: Good communicator, occasionally over-talks or misses cues.
- 6-10: Adequate but wouldn't differentiate in a sales conversation.
- 0-5: Communication would hinder their ability to sell.

### Sales-Specific Green Flags
- `numbers_ready` — Knows their metrics cold, shares specific data without hesitation
- `process_driven` — Has a clear methodology, can describe their sales process step by step
- `competitive_awareness` — Understands their market and can articulate positioning
- `coachable` — Actively seeks feedback, talks about applying coaching
- `customer_obsessed` — Genuinely curious about customer problems, not just quotas
- `resilient` — Bounces back from losses with specific lessons learned
- `multi_threader` — Builds relationships across the buying committee

### Sales-Specific Red Flags
- `numbers_evasive` — Can't or won't share quota attainment, hedges on metrics
- `blames_territory` — Every miss is the territory's fault, the leads' fault, the product's fault
- `feature_pitcher` — Leads with product features instead of customer discovery
- `lone_wolf` — Resists process, CRM discipline, team collaboration
- `all_charm_no_substance` — Great rapport but can't describe methodology or specific examples
- `money_only` — Sole motivation is compensation with no interest in the product, customers, or craft
- `quota_chaser` — Short-term thinking, gaming metrics at expense of customer relationships

## Seniority Calibration

**SDR/BDR (entry-level):**
- Weight coachability and resilience over methodology
- Look for hunger, competitiveness, and organization
- Accept that they won't have a polished sales process yet
- Ask about volume: outreach cadence, meetings booked, conversion rates

**Account Executive (mid-level):**
- Expect clear quota attainment history with specific numbers
- Should describe a complete deal cycle with real examples
- Should have objection handling stories that show adaptation
- Look for discovery depth — do they sell value or features?

**Senior AE / Enterprise:**
- Expect multi-stakeholder, complex deal experience
- Should describe 6-12+ month deal cycles with strategic maneuvering
- Should have executive-level communication skills
- Look for strategic account planning, not just deal-by-deal selling

## Cross-Cartridge Notes

If a sales candidate has management experience:
- Ask: "Are you looking to stay in an individual contributor sales role, or move into sales leadership?"
- If leadership → supplement with leadership cartridge questions about coaching and team building
- If IC → note the management background, stay in sales cartridge

If a sales candidate comes from a technical background:
- Note as a strength — technical sellers are valuable in complex sales
- Don't redirect to technical cartridge; capture the technical depth as a competitive advantage
