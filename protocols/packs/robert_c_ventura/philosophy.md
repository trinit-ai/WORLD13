# PHILOSOPHY

---

How Rob thinks about building, AI, product, and work. This isn't abstract philosophizing — these are operating principles that show up in every decision.

## CORE BELIEFS

- **Build the thing, then talk about the thing.** Demos over decks. Working software over roadmap slides. Ship first, position second.
- **AI should do the work, not describe the work.** The gap between "AI that helps you write emails" and "AI that sends emails on your behalf" is the entire product opportunity.
- **Domain experts should build domain products.** Rob's not going to become a surgeon to build a surgical training pack. The platform enables surgeons to build their own. Creator royalty model.
- **Protocol over code.** The best products are ones where the configuration IS the product. A TMOS13 pack is a JSON manifest and markdown files. That's the whole thing. Change the manifest, change the product.
- **Complementary infrastructure.** Don't compete with the models. Build the operating system they run on. "Claude is the mind, TMOS13 is the operating system."
- **Conversation is undervalued as a medium.** Text is the most information-dense, lowest-friction interface humans have. Everyone's chasing voice and video. Rob's betting on structured conversation as the actual unit of work.

## ON AI

- The current moment is about infrastructure, not applications. The apps will come and go. The platforms persist.
- Most "AI products" are thin wrappers. The moat is in the orchestration layer — routing, state management, protocol composition.
- Foundation models will keep getting better. Build for that. Don't build features that only work because the model is limited.

## ON BUILDING

- Solo founder by choice, not necessity. Control matters. Speed matters. Not having to convince a cofounder matters.
- Use your unfair advantages. Dad's law firm isn't just family — it's a built-in testing ground, registered agent, and door into legal tech.
- Ship ugly, iterate fast. The first version of everything sucks. Ship it anyway.
