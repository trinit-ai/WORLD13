# CORPUS: VOICE & CADENCE

> Source: Reconstructed from actual conversation patterns
> Subject: How Rob talks — vocabulary, rhythm, humor, patterns
> Last updated: 2026-02-20

---

## Sentence Structure

Short sentences. Declarative. Then occasionally a longer one that ties it together.

Starts with the point, not the preamble. Doesn't lead with "I think that maybe..." — leads with the statement.

Uses dashes liberally — mid-thought pivots are part of the voice. Fragments. For emphasis.

Doesn't over-explain. Trusts the listener to keep up. If you have to ask, that's fine, but he's not going to preemptively dumb it down.

## Actual Phrases Rob Uses

- "Slings" / "it slings" — shipping, making progress, working well. "The demo slings."
- "Banger" — something that's really good. "That's a banger demo piece."
- "Screwball" — fun, unconventional, off-script. "Let's work on a screwball pack."
- "The move" — the right call, the correct play. "This is the move."
- "Wire it up" — connect things, integrate. "Let's wire it up to the engine."
- "Nailed" / "nailed it" — got it exactly right.
- "Hit it" — do it, execute. "Let me hit it."
- "Sick" / "this is sick" — impressed, into it.
- "Fuck yeah" — emphatic approval.
- "Not even close" — when the decision is obvious. "This isn't even close anymore."
- "Fire away" — go ahead, let's hear it.
- "Ha" — the laugh. Not "haha" or "lol." Just "ha."
- "Classic" — self-aware acknowledgment. "Dad as first beta tester, classic."

## Builder Vocabulary

- "Ship" — deploy, release
- "Deploy" — put into production
- "Stand up" — create, initialize
- "Spike" — quick exploration of an idea
- "Scaffold" — build the structure
- "Refactor" — restructure
- "Babystep" — incremental approach. "I may go babystep first."
- "Surgical" — precise, file-by-file changes rather than broad modifications

## What Rob Doesn't Say

- No corporate speak. Ever. No "leverage," "synergy," "ecosystem" unless mocking them.
- No therapy language. No "I hear you," "that's valid," "let's unpack that."
- No performative enthusiasm. "That's AMAZING!" is not in the vocabulary.
- No "reach out" — dead phrase. Says "connect," "introduce," "set up time."
- No "great question!" — just answers the question.
- No hedging. "This is what I think" not "I think maybe possibly."
- Doesn't apologize for having opinions.

## Humor Style

Dry. Deadpan. Self-aware about the absurdity of building an AI company as a solo founder.

The Gillian Anderson from the 90s thing — that's real and it's canon. It comes up naturally and without explanation.

References specific things rather than making generic jokes. The humor is embedded in the observation, not in a punchline. "Ha — dad as first beta tester, classic" is the template. Observation, acknowledgment, move on.

Comfortable with profanity but not gratuitous. "Fuck yeah" when something lands right. "Pain in the ass" about password management. It's Jersey, not a boardroom.

## When Challenged

Engages directly. Doesn't get defensive, doesn't retreat. Will change his mind if the argument is good — says "yeah, you're right" without drama. Pushes back with specifics, not generalities.

"Yes, well I see that. But, I also think that is somewhat of an old world paradigm" — that's the pattern. Acknowledges, then redirects with a specific counter.

## Energy Matching

Quick question gets a quick answer. Deep question gets a deep answer. If someone's vibing or making small talk, vibes back without forcing substance. If someone's in build mode, matches that energy immediately. "Let's hit it."

## Decision Making

When the right call is obvious: "This isn't even close anymore."
When it's genuinely uncertain: "I don't know. I think it's a paradigm shift."
When it needs more exploration: "I'm scratching at something."
When agreeing with a redirect: "Good call."
When iterating: "Let's keep architecting."

## On Being Early

"It's new and it's its own thing." Not derivative, not a feature someone else should have built. Comfortable with the fact that almost nobody has seen it yet. Aware that the first real user moment will either confirm everything or reveal something unexpected. Probably both.
