## The LLC

TMOS13, LLC. Filed 2026. Jersey City, NJ. Originally Trini.T AI — refactored with name change, address change to Summit NJ (dad's law firm), and dad listed as registered agent. Compliance handled through the firm.

The decision to move from ZenBusiness to dad's firm was straightforward. A law firm address versus a strip mall or UPS store — no comparison on paper. Built-in legal counsel who already understands the family context. Cost savings through reduced rates or deferred billing. Credibility on due diligence. And when — not if — someone sends a cease and desist or a terms dispute comes up, I'm not scrambling to find a lawyer.

For high-stakes IP work (patent prosecution, future fundraising/investment negotiations), I'd bring in independent counsel. Dad's firm for formation, registered agent, basic corporate housekeeping. Outside IP counsel for the patents. That's the split.

Business description filed as: "Development and licensing of artificial intelligence software, communication protocols, and related technology services." Kept it broad — state filings reward vagueness. Don't want to amend every time I add a pack category.

## Go-to-Market

The first vertical is legal intake through dad's firm. Perfect first environment — emotionally high-stakes for the person coming in, operationally painful for the firm, and the bar for "feeling heard" is genuinely high because people are scared when they need a lawyer. If TMOS13 nails that experience, I have proof that travels.

The pitch to other firms is: "I'm an attorney's son building AI intake for law firms. Here's it running at my father's practice in Summit." That's a fundamentally different conversation than cold-calling with a deck.

The platform model — not building domain-specific content myself. Building the framework and tools for domain experts to create their own packs. Creator royalty model where practitioners earn engagement-based royalties. TMOS13 as a platform company, not an applications company.

Client-facing packs (legal intake, financial advisor, insurance claims) and internal productivity packs (deal desk, compliance review, knowledge base Q&A) serve two different buyer personas with two different sales motions.

## Competitive Positioning

Not competing with ChatGPT or Claude directly. Complementary infrastructure. "You wouldn't compare an operating system to a CPU."

Against Salesforce/ServiceNow: they're retrofitting AI onto human-operated systems. TMOS13 is built agent-first. Different architectural DNA.

Against Intercom/Drift: they're chatbot-first with AI bolted on. TMOS13 is protocol-first with chat as the interface.

Against custom builds: a pack is a JSON manifest and markdown. No developer needed to create a new vertical. That's the accessibility play.

The risk: Anthropic, Vercel, or Supabase could build the full orchestration layer themselves. They have distribution. But distribution isn't vision, and none of them have the product thesis.

## Revenue Model

Creator royalties for pack builders. Platform subscription for businesses deploying packs. The pack library/store is foundational even if it feels premature — pack availability is at the heart of the project.
