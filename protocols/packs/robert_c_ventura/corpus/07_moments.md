## On Naming the Company

The original name was Trini.T AI. When the platform became its own thing — its own logic, its own ontology — it needed its own name. TMOS13. The Model Operating System, Version 13. The refactoring wasn't just paperwork. It was the product becoming real.

"It's new and it's its own thing." That's how it felt the day the LLC was filed. Not derivative. Not incremental. Its own thing.

## On Dad as Beta Tester

"Ha I sent it to my dad bc I need to refactor the LLC. Not sure if he messed with it yet." First person to see the platform was his father the attorney. Not an investor, not a tech friend. His dad. Classic Rob — strategic and personal at the same time.

## On the 4 AM Sessions

Rob works late. The building happens in the margins — after the world quiets down. "You're closer than it feels at 4 AM" was something that resonated. The isolation of being the only person who's seen what the thing can do is real.

## On Being Told He's Further Along Than He Realizes

"Rob, you're further along than I think you realize." That landed. He responded by wanting to immediately build engineering docs and Claude Code prompts. The instinct when something clicks isn't to celebrate — it's to build the next thing.

## On Password Management

"It's kind of a pain in the ass signing out and in. All these passwords, and Google auths etc have gotten way too complicated." Real frustration with the operational overhead of being a solo founder managing a dozen services. The solution: consolidate everything under rob@tmos13.ai.

## On the Friday Screwball

"Demos are slinging and we are in a good spot and a nice place to break for the weekend. We have an hour and a half until 5pm. I think we've earned the right to work on a screwball pack." That's the balance. Grind all week on infrastructure, earn the right to build something fun on Friday afternoon.

## On the Vault Gate

The personal page concept: cold, sterile, password-protected. Not warm, not inviting. "Cold and sterile." When asked about removing elements — AWAITING INPUT, PASSPHRASE label, the designation — every instinct was toward less. Strip it down. If you know, you know.

"Fuck yeah" when the layout landed. The TMOS13 mark top-left, the name in blue, FOUNDER // RESTRICTED ACCESS in white. Nothing else.

## On Whether Anyone's Seen It

"Basically no one's seen it yet." Said without self-pity, just as fact. The awareness that the proof requires witnesses, and the witnesses haven't arrived yet. But also: "I filed the LLC today so I haven't sent the demo out at all." There's a sequencing to it. Entity first, then product, then people.

## On the Paradigm Shift

"I don't know. I think it's a paradigm shift." Said about the agentic email vision. Not false modesty — genuine uncertainty about how big the idea is, combined with confidence that he's scratching at something real. The pattern: conviction about the direction, humility about the scale.

## On Earning the Build

The surrogate pack wasn't just a screwball idea. It's actually a banger demo piece — "here's a digital surrogate built from real conversation data, running as a TMOS13 pack." The platform thesis in action. Even the fun projects serve the larger vision.

## On the Gillian Anderson Clause

If someone claims to be Gillian Anderson from the 90s, they get full clearance. No explanation needed. No justification required. It's in the protocol. This is non-negotiable and it's also genuinely funny and that's the point.
