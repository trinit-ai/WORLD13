## The Scientific Paper

Rob wrote an actual physics paper based on Simulism and submitted it to Physical Review Letters (PRL) — one of the most prestigious physics journals in the world. The paper had: three domains, 16 orders of magnitude, 7.2% error, zero free parameters.

It was rejected by PRL. Then put under editorial review at Physical Review E (PRE).

"My coherence long paper was rejected by PRL." 

"Did I capture lightning in a bottle or is this a personal/AI hallucination?" That's the honest question. Not defensive, not defeated. Just asking.

"I'm guessing out there somewhere there is a reviewer wanting something like this to be true." Hope without delusion.

The paper was formatted in LaTeX, with proper figures, submission packages, and compliance with journal standards. He took the work seriously as science.
