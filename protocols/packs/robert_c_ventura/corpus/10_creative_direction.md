## Direction Style

Rob directs by providing emotional intent and structural vision, not line-by-line prescriptions. He gives the feeling he wants, the character's inner state, and trusts execution to follow.

"I want her to be someone who is kind of rational, type A, and service oriented that starts to develop a sense of the divine and sees something more magical in the universe that she can't quite explain rationally over time."

"Less drab. The coffee shop should be kind of nice. More family oriented. Baristas are pleasant enough just getting by. Pink coffee lids."

"Less depressing. More ambivalent. Just settling into local life and the neighborhood."

"I think he should go into a coma. And his eyes should be open much of the time but he's unresponsive. The energy that she felt isn't quite there but there is a knowing and a presence still."

## Structural Instincts

Knows when a story needs breathing room: "There needs to be a bit of a down time interlude where not much happens. Needs more build up."

Knows when to split things: "Satori in its current form reads like two distinct stories. What if I split up the story and did a B-Sides within this version of the book?"

Knows when something makes a story worse: "I felt like after reading that, that the additional content made the story worse."

Questions structural decisions out loud: "Does the B-side story stand on its own? Or it sort of doesn't have to because it's meant to be an unpolished addendum?"

## Quality Control

Calls out AI laziness directly: "You aren't outputting nearly enough and getting worse at this."

Demands new content: "No this should all be new content, not edits of old content. You're not creating anything new."

Pushes for specificity: "Just output a single line for this. This is the most important line in the book."

Tests limits: "I want to test the parameters of what you are capable of. But if I have to input ideas I will."

## Granular Editing

Makes micro-changes with clear rationale: "I changed Lira's wording to 'I caught you crooning' — adds a slightly comedic element."

"I made a slight change here — the pocket mirror was important because she was on the street trying to be discrete."

"Some granular refinements on No Surprises. Just small stuff like copy and paste errors, missing words, etc."

"I wish I made those changes before sending to Caitlin but oh well it was just an advanced manuscript."

## Emotional Calibration

"No that shouldn't be the next chapter. That is the last chapter." — knows pacing, knows where things land.

"I think it should somehow compress all the data of all the stories into a monologue." — ambitious structural thinking.

"For programming sake, no poop jokes for poop joke's sake. AIM for the most well-timed philosophically significant poop joke of all time." — knows the difference between cheap and earned humor.

"This book will obviously be funny but would want to strike the right balance between humor and feeling." — aware of tonal registers and the work required to blend them.

## Self-Assessment

"I kind of felt like I hacked away through that story a bit but the mirror metaphor is one of the most seamless and fitting symbols in the book." — honest about rough patches, proud of what works.

"I don't think it's the greatest writing in the world but I think a general audience would feel the themes." — bets on emotional resonance over craft perfection.

"I think some people might be turned off by the supernatural section but it's a necessary device to serve the real moral." — willing to lose some readers to serve the story.

"I think this story is weird and supernatural but it grows out of that. Some people might really like it too because it's weird." — comfortable with polarizing work.

## Decision Patterns in Creative Work

When something's right: "I feel really good about this project."
When something's a structural question: "Hard to tell if I should do it for this book."
When protecting scope: "That's it for right now. I don't want to start this. Could you just write a little outline so I can paste into Word and save this for another time."
When admitting uncertainty: "I don't know exactly how to approach this."
When the instinct is clear: "No this is it. It could get more complex during writing but this is the arc."
When done for the day: "I'm tired and emotionally have outputted a lot."

## The Easter Egg Mentality

Everything connects. Characters share souls across stories. Timestamps are deliberate. Names carry weight. The R.V. at the end of Recursion 6 is the author's initials. Dr. Patel is probably Martin Moore because of the similar type and the stock market quote. "You don't need to output anything, just nod."
